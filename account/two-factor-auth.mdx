---
title: "Two-Factor Auth"
description: "Enable two-factor authentication to secure your account."
---

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of verification to your sign-in process. After you enable it, anyone signing into your account must provide both your password and a one-time passcode (OTP) sent to your registered email address — making unauthorized access significantly harder.

<Tip>
  Enabling 2FA is optional but strongly recommended. It provides multi-layered security for your Zeblok Computational account.
</Tip>

## Enable 2FA

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to the dashboard">
    Enter your credentials on the [Sign In](https://app.zbl-aws.zeblok.com/) page. Once authenticated, you will be redirected to your dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the account menu">
    Click the three-dot menu in the top-right corner of the dashboard. A dropdown menu will appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle 2FA on">
    At the bottom of the dropdown, click **2-FA**. Two-factor authentication will be enabled for your account immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Enabling 2FA takes effect the next time anyone attempts to sign in to your account. Your current session is not affected.
</Note>

## Sign In with 2FA Enabled

Once 2FA is active, the sign-in flow includes an additional OTP verification step.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your credentials">
    Go to the sign-in page, enter your email and password, and click **Sign In**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your email for the OTP">
    An OTP will be sent to your registered email address. Open the email from Zeblok Computational and copy the code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the OTP">
    Enter the one-time passcode on the verification screen and submit. If the code is valid, you will be signed in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The OTP is valid for **5 minutes** only. You have **4 attempts** to enter a valid OTP — after 4 failed attempts your account will be locked and you will need to contact a Zeblok Administrator to regain access.
</Warning>

## Disable 2FA

To turn off two-factor authentication, open the three-dot menu in the top-right corner of the dashboard and click **2-FA** again. The toggle will disable 2FA for your account, taking effect at the next sign-in.
