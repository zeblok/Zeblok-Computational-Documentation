## Overview

Independent Software Vendors (ISVs) can onboard their own custom microservices and workstations directly into the Zeblok AI-MicroCloud. Once onboarded, these resources appear alongside the platform's built-in offerings and can be spawned by users in any authorized namespace — just like first-party workstations and microservices.

ISV onboarding covers two primary resource types:

- **Custom MicroServices** — containerized services packaged as Docker images that you bring to the platform
- **Custom Ai-WorkStations** — JupyterLab-based notebook environments built from your own container images

## Onboarding Process

To onboard a microservice, you register a container image in the Ai-MicroCloud admin panel by providing the image tag, compatible plans, internal port definitions, and optional configuration. To onboard a workstation, you supply the image tag, run command, compatible plans, and workstation type classification. Detailed field definitions for both forms are available in the Spawn Ai-WorkStation and Spawn MicroService guides.

<Info>
  For the full ISV onboarding guide and document, contact the Zeblok team.
</Info>

## kubectl Access for ISV Admins

If you are an ISV admin user and need direct cluster access — for example, to debug a running container or manage Kubernetes resources in your namespace — Zeblok can provision scoped `kubectl` access restricted to your specific namespace. Contact the Zeblok team to request this access.

## What Happens After Onboarding

Once your microservice or workstation image is registered and marked as publicly accessible, platform users can spawn it through the standard **Spawn MicroService** or **Spawn Ai-WorkStation** workflows. You can also attach compatible plans to control which resource tiers are available to users who select your image.
